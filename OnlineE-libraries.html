Introduction to Algorithms

written by Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, and Clifford Stein

"Introduction to Algorithms" is a comprehensive and rigorous text that covers a broad range of algorithms in depth, making their design and analysis accessible to all readers. The book includes self-contained chapters and algorithms in pseudocode. Widely regarded as the leading algorithms text in universities and a standard reference for professionals, the fourth edition has been thoroughly updated with new material on matchings in bipartite graphs, online algorithms, machine learning, and other topics, maintaining its status as an essential resource for understanding algorithms.

Algorithms

written by Robert Sedgewick and Kevin Wayne

The fourth edition of Robert Sedgewick and Kevin Wayne's "Algorithms" is a leading textbook widely used in colleges and universities globally. It covers essential computer algorithms, providing comprehensive treatment of data structures, sorting, searching, graph processing, and string processing, including fifty key algorithms every programmer should know. This edition introduces new Java implementations in an accessible modular programming style, with all code exposed and ready for use.

Basic and Clinical Pharmacology 15e

written by Bertram G. Katzung; Anthony J. Trevor

"Basic and Clinical Pharmacology" is a comprehensive, full-color guide with hundreds of illustrations, trusted by students for decades. Reflecting typical pharmacology course sequences, it covers essential concepts in pharmacology and clinical practice applications. The latest edition features extensive updates, including expanded sections on transporters, pharmacogenomics, and new drugs. This engaging guide remains an essential resource for mastering the science and clinical application of pharmacology.

Mechanical Engineering Design

written by Richard G. Budynas, J. Keith Nisbeth

"Shigley's Mechanical Engineering Design" is ideal for students beginning mechanical engineering design, guiding them through design decisions and industrial standards. It combines a focus on fundamentals with a modern emphasis on design and new applications, maintaining the approach that has made it a standard for nearly 50 years. The optional McGraw-Hill Education's Connect system enhances learning by adapting to students' needs, allowing professors to easily assign and grade homework, quizzes, and tests. Connect features randomized problems and multi-step solutions to support effective learning and prevent answer sharing.

Think and Grow Rich

written by by Napoleon Hill, Bill Hartley, Ann Hartley, Ben Holden-Crowther

Think and Grow Rich has been called the “Granddaddy of All Motivational Literature.” It was the first book to boldly ask, “What makes a winner?” The man who asked and listened for the answer, Napoleon Hill, is now counted in the top ranks of the world's winners himself. The most famous of all teachers of success spent “a fortune and the better part of a lifetime of effort” to produce the “Law of Success” philosophy that forms the basis of his books and that is so powerfully summarized in this one.

The Art of Electronics

written by Paul Horowitz and Winfield Hil

The thoroughly revised third edition of "The Art of Electronics" remains the authoritative book on electronic circuit design. This edition features new and enhanced topics, 90 oscilloscope screenshots, dozens of graphs with essential measured data, and 80 tables listing characteristics of 1650 active components for intelligent component selection. It retains the informal and accessible style of earlier editions, making it indispensable for students, researchers, professionals, and amateurs working with electronic circuits. The book continues to be the gold standard reference in the field

The Elegant Universe

written by Brian Greene

In "The Elegant Universe," Brian Greene, a leading string theorist, provides a captivating and accessible explanation of string theory. Greene reveals a universe of 11 dimensions where space fabric tears and repairs itself, and all matter is generated by vibrating loops of energy. Using vivid analogies, such as amusement park rides and ants on a garden hose, Greene makes complex physics both understandable and engaging. Praised for its scientific insight and elegant writing, the book is a brilliant and entertaining journey through modern physics, bringing readers closer to understanding the universe's workings.

Practical Electronics for Inventors

written by Paul Scherz and Simon Monk

"Practical Electronics for Inventors, Fourth Edition," written by experienced engineers and hobbyists, helps readers advance their electronics knowledge and develop functioning gadgets. The book provides step-by-step instructions, schematics, and illustrations, covering component selection, circuit design and construction, microcontrollers, ICs, software tools, and testing techniques. It includes new instructions on programmable logic, semiconductors, operational amplifiers, voltage regulators, power supplies, and digital electronics. This accessible guide is essential for anyone looking to build and refine their electronics skills.

Clean Code

written by Robert C. Martin

.

"Clean Code" by Robert C. Martin offers a revolutionary approach to software development by emphasizing the importance of clean, well-written code. It highlights the negative impact of poorly written code on development organizations and provides practical strategies for improving code quality. Through principles, patterns, and case studies, readers learn to identify and address common issues in code, ultimately becoming better programmers. The book encourages a shift in mindset towards software craftsmanship and provides valuable insights and heuristics for writing, reading, and maintaining clean code. It serves as an invaluable resource for software developers striving for professionalism and excellence in their craft.
